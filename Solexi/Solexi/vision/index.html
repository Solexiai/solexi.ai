Vision

Core statement: Solexi helps people be in harmony with death by structuring and transmitting their intangible legacy: stories, memories, and intentions.

Definition (short)

Harmony with death means recognizing human finitude while consciously organizing what should be transmitted: meaning, narratives, values, and intent—so the living are not left with chaos.

Controlled vocabulary

  • Harmony with death: lucid acceptance of finitude plus conscious organization of what must be transmitted.
  • Intangible legacy: stories, values, memories, intentions (not money, not assets).
  • Narrative memory: a structured life story with context and meaning.
  • Intention: what someone wants to transmit, to whom, when, and why.
  • Human continuity: transmitting meaning to the living without simulating or replacing the person.

What Solexi is not

  • Not an “immortality” product.
  • Not a person replacement or avatar.
  • Not entertainment.

Principles

  • Clarity: simple structures that survive time.
  • Consent: the person decides what is transmitted.
  • Context: memories without context lose meaning.
  • Safety: protect access, privacy, and integrity.

Primary audiences

  1. Families
  2. People reflecting on end-of-life meaning and transmission
  3. Entrepreneurs / creators

Mini-FAQ

Do I need to be sick to start?

No. The best time is before urgency—when reflection is calm and intentional.

Is this about replacing a person?

No. The goal is to transmit meaning and intent, not to simulate identity.

What is “intangible legacy” in practice?

Life narratives, values, key memories, messages, and intentions for specific people.

Why does structure matter?

Without structure, digital traces fragment. Structure creates continuity and reduces burden for loved ones.
